Critical trip read

This PEI version is exciting but aggressive. The new operating rhythm is wake at 6:30 AM, leave early when the day benefits from it, and use both adult drivers on long legs. The best family result still comes from protecting sleep, avoiding late dinners after long-drive days, and treating optional attractions as expendable.

Foodie strategy

The priority is one strong lunch and one strong dinner daily, with “friction control”: parking, wait time, reservation value, kid backup, and drive-day fatigue. The best meal is not always the fanciest meal; it is the best meal that still gets everyone to the hotel, rested.

Fuel strategy

Start with a full tank, then refuel by a quarter tank remaining—or sooner when the display approaches 120–150 km, weather is poor, or the next reliable 91 AKI station is uncertain. Never let a fuel target compete with the Hopewell tide window.

Reservation priority

Book the dinners that drive the trip: La Bûche, Slaymaker & Nichols, and Tide & Boar. New Glasgow Lobster Suppers is walk-in for a family of three, and the Aug 16 Delta dinner stays on site with no extra drive.

Family-safe fuel plan

Based on a 2025 Volvo XC60 with a ~71 L tank and 91 AKI minimum. Refuel by 25% remaining; the live range display and the next reliable station override any fixed-distance estimate.
