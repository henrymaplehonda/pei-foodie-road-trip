Critical trip read

This PEI version is exciting but aggressive. The best family result comes from protecting sleep, avoiding late dinners after long-drive days, and treating optional attractions as expendable. The food plan is intentionally high-quality but not formal every meal, because a 6-year-old changes the real definition of “best.”

Foodie strategy

The priority is one strong lunch and one strong dinner daily, with “friction control”: parking, wait time, reservation value, kid backup, and drive-day fatigue. The best meal is not always the fanciest meal; it is the best meal that still gets everyone to the hotel, rested.

Fuel strategy

Use Esso where practical, fill with 91 AKI premium, and refuel around the 1/4-tank point. Do not enter rural NB / PEI segments with a low tank, especially after dinner or before attractions when everyone is tired.

Reservation priority

Book or confirm the dinners that drive the trip: La Bûche, Wolastoq Wharf, New Glasgow Lobster Suppers, Slaymaker & Nichols, and Tide & Boar. For lunch, protect speed unless the day is light.

Esso fuel plan

Based on 2025 Volvo XC60, premium recommended 91 AKI, ~71 L tank, and conservative 800 km highway range.
